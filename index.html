01 · About

I never planned to become a data scientist.

My academic journey began in architecture, where I developed a deep appreciation for design thinking, spatial reasoning, and solving complex real-world problems. Architecture taught me that every challenge has patterns waiting to be understood — it just requires the right perspective.

As I worked with increasingly complex planning and design problems, I became fascinated by the power of data to uncover those patterns. I realized that combining analytical thinking with technology could help solve challenges far beyond the built environment. That realization led me to transition into data science.

Since then, I have built expertise in data analytics, machine learning, visualization, and predictive modeling through intensive training, open-source collaboration, and practical projects. Today, my work sits at the intersection of data science and geospatial analytics, where I explore how spatial intelligence and machine learning can improve decision-making in infrastructure planning, climate resilience, public health, and humanitarian response.

I believe the most impactful data science doesn't just produce accurate models — it helps people make better decisions.

"The value of data lies not in its volume, but in our ability to transform it into reliable insights that solve real-world problems."
— my working philosophy

P·01 Urban Heat & Vulnerable Populations — Lagos GeoPandasRasterioRemote Sensing →

The Challenge

Lagos is heating up, but nobody knew precisely which neighbourhoods carry the highest combined burden of extreme land surface temperature and vulnerable residents.

Why It Matters

Heat kills quietly — especially the elderly, children, and low-income communities in dense informal settlements. Targeted adaptation needs targeted maps.

My Approach

Combined satellite-derived land surface temperature with population and demographic indicators in Python, building a reproducible pipeline to score and map heat-vulnerability hotspots.

Results

Thematic hotspot maps identifying high-risk zones, presented at the Gendering AI Conference in Nairobi (2025) to inform climate adaptation and urban planning discussions.

Lessons Learned

Spatial resolution is a policy decision, not just a technical one — the scale you map at determines who becomes visible in the data.

P·02 Land Use × SDGs Across 11 Countries — Outreachy / Moja Global PythonGeoPandasOpen Source →

The Challenge

Land sector data across countries is messy, inconsistent, and rarely connected to the global goals it should inform.

Why It Matters

Governments estimating greenhouse gas emissions from forestry and agriculture need clean, comparable geospatial evidence to drive climate policy.

My Approach

Processed and analyzed geospatial datasets for 11 countries with Python (GeoPandas, Rasterio), mapping land-use attributes directly to relevant Sustainable Development Goals, and documented every workflow for the community.

Results

Interactive notebooks and maps used by stakeholders to guide environmental sustainability policy, plus documentation that improved onboarding for new open-source contributors.

Lessons Learned

In open source, reproducibility is generosity — code that only runs on your machine helps no one.

P·03 Loan Default Risk Prediction — 307K Applicants Classificationscikit-learnImbalanced Data →

The Challenge

Predict which loan applicants are likely to default, using the Home Credit dataset — 307,511 applicants described by 122 features, with heavily imbalanced classes.

Why It Matters

Every false negative is money lost; every false positive is a creditworthy customer turned away. Risk models decide who gets access to finance.

My Approach

Full ML workflow: business framing, missing-value strategy across 122 features, categorical encoding, train/validation/test splits, model training and threshold-aware evaluation focused on the business cost of each error type.

Results

A documented, end-to-end classification pipeline weighing precision against recall in business terms, with clear recommendations for lending decisions.

Lessons Learned

With imbalanced data, accuracy is a vanity metric — the confusion matrix is where the real conversation happens.

↗ GitHub repo
P·04 House Price Prediction — Regularized Regression Ridge/LassoGridSearchCVR² 0.855 →

The Challenge

Estimate residential sale prices from 80 property features so a real estate company can price listings on evidence instead of gut feeling.

Why It Matters

Overpricing means unsold inventory; underpricing means lost revenue — a systematic $10K underestimate across 50 sales is half a million dollars gone.

My Approach

Compared OLS, Ridge, and Lasso regression with GridSearchCV tuning, full diagnostic analysis (residuals, Q-Q plots, learning curves), and feature selection on the Kaggle House Prices dataset.

Results

Ridge Regression won with R² of 0.855 and RMSE of ~$34K on the held-out test set, plus ranked value-driver recommendations for agents (quality, living area, garage capacity).

Lessons Learned

Regularization is humility encoded in math — admitting your features overlap makes your predictions stronger.

↗ GitHub repo
P·05 Urban Green Space Mapping — Data Science Nigeria Mentorship Satellite ImagerySustainability →

The Challenge

Assess how urban green space is distributed — and who has access to it — by fusing satellite imagery with socio-environmental indicators.

Why It Matters

Green space is climate infrastructure: it cools cities, cleans air, and supports wellbeing. Unequal distribution is an equity problem you can see from orbit.

My Approach

Cleaned and validated multi-source datasets, integrated satellite imagery with ground indicators, and produced analysis supporting restoration and sustainability planning.

Results

A validated multi-source dataset and green-space distribution assessment supporting sustainability analysis and restoration planning.

Lessons Learned

Data fusion is 80% making sources agree with each other — projections, resolutions, and dates rarely align on their own.
